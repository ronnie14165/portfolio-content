About Me

I am a second year student majoring in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Umich. I love photography when I was young. I am going to show more contents about photography with you in this website. My past experience: I participated an underwater wireless sensing project named EPSRC USMART collaborated by Newcastle University, Heriot-Watt University and the University of York under the guidance of my tutor Dr. Mauro Dragone. My responsibility is to carry out communication with about 1,000 underwater sensors and optical communication core equipment with Lora Technology, and encrypt and transmit the transmission information through OTAA protocol (over the air activation). TDMA protocol is used to help Lora Gateway on the water better allocate time and avoid information conflicts, maximizing the hardware advantage to send commands from the terminal and upload the data obtained from the sensor to an IOT platform called thingsboard through MQTT protocol. By participating in this experiment, I understood the operating mode of OTAA, ABP and other protocols, and used MicroPython to program Lora Gateway and development board composed of Pytrack and Lopy4. At the same time, I learned how to effectively use the dashboard of IOT platform and how to get data from IOT platform through data mining. In addition, I also used Python to achieve MQTT and other algorithms.

I used to design a robot for entertainment venues together with other five team members. Acted as the team leader and C++ engineer, I undertook a majority of responsibilities, including the design of the state machine, serial communications and different user modes. In our design, the development board uses the serial communication to call the user-defined function to read the color sensor, infrared sensor and distance sensor other sensors to judge the game process and drive the motor accurately according to the process. After realizing the reading and calling of basic sensor and servo, I added user mode (including easy mode and hard mode) and maintain mode for better user experience. We experienced some setbacks in the process, such as inoperative sensor and breakdown of power supplies, but through repeated debugging and persistent trying, we successfully complete it. In the final assessment, our product ranked in the first place among 97 competitors.
